Research

Current Dissertation Research

Predicting Employee Intention to Stay: A Comparative Study of Multivariate Machine Learning Models and PLS-SEM

University of Wolverhampton • Supervised by Prof. Dr. Tahir Mahmood

This research investigates employee retention in Vietnam's electronics manufacturing sector through a hybrid analytical framework combining Machine Learning algorithms with Partial Least Squares Structural Equation Modeling (PLS-SEM).

The study analyzes data from 604 employees across 29 variables, examining relationships between organizational factors including Perceived Organizational Support, Job Satisfaction, and Work-Life Balance with employees' intention to stay.

Key Findings

β = 0.600 Organizational Support Effect
β = 0.463 Work-Life Balance Effect
604 Employees Analyzed
7 ML Algorithms Compared

Methodology

  • Comparative analysis of 7 machine learning algorithms
  • PLS-SEM for structural relationship modeling
  • SHAP analysis for feature importance interpretation
  • Interactive Streamlit dashboard for result visualization
